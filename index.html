About me

I am currently exploring new opportunities and am open to new career possibilities.

Previously as an assistant professor of Data Science in the Faculty of Science and Technology at Beijing Normal University - Hong Kong Baptist University United International College, I specialize in researching the evolving dynamics of digital media platforms. My focus is on the study of human-machine social behavior with an emphasis on social information and language processing.

I earned my PhD in Information Science from Drexel University College of Computing & Informatics, under the mentorship of Dr. Tony Hu. My research involves using digital media data to derive insights, with a particular focus on social information and language processing. I frequently collaborate with experts in a variety of fields, including sociology, computer science, health and clinical informatics, communications, and political science.

When I'm not working, I enjoy traveling and photography.
